# Phase 2D: Support and Infrastructure Companies - Research Summary

**Date**: February 14, 2026
**Status**: Research Complete - Profiles In Progress
**Total Companies**: 26 profiled

## Executive Summary

Phase 2D focuses on profiling 26 support and infrastructure companies critical to the bioplastics industry ecosystem. These companies provide essential technologies, testing, certification, research, and enabling services across the value chain. Unlike Phase 1 (major global producers) and Phases 2A-2C (bioplastic manufacturers), Phase 2D companies enable and support the bioplastics industry through specialized services and infrastructure.

## Company Categories

### Technology Companies (22 total)

These companies develop materials science innovations, production technologies, and enabling solutions:

#### Enzymatic Recycling & Chemical Technology
- **CARBIOS** (France) - PET biorecycling through enzymatic depolymerization; pilot plant operational, commercial plant planned with Indorama Ventures
- **BioBTX** (Netherlands) - Chemical recycling of mixed plastic waste to renewable aromatics (BTX); €80M+ funded, commercial PETRA plant targeting late 2026
- **Uhde Inventa-Fischer** (Germany) - Engineering company specializing in PLA production technology; PLAneo® technology development

#### Fermentation & Biotechnology
- **BIOWEG** (Germany) - Bacterial cellulose production via fermentation for microplastic alternatives; €16M Series A, partnership with Bayer
- **Renaissance BioScience** (Canada) - Yeast bioengineering for fermentation-based bioplastic building blocks; partnership with Biome Bioplastics
- **TripleW** (Israel) - Food waste conversion to lactic acid (Circulac®) and PLA bioplastics; €16.5M Series B, world's first food waste PLA achieved Sept 2025

#### Cellulose & Natural Fiber Technology
- **Lenzing AG** (Austria) - World-leading cellulose fiber producer; TENCEL®, VEOCEL™ brands; investment in TreeToTextile AB
- **TreeToTextile AB** (Sweden) - Next-generation cellulose fiber technology from sustainably managed forests; 70-90% emission reduction; Lenzing majority investment
- **Blue Ocean Closures** (Sweden) - Fiber-based closure systems from cellulose; 9x carbon reduction; partnerships with Absolut and Coca-Cola

#### Circular Economy & Ocean-Safe Materials
- **OceanSafe** (Switzerland) - Ocean-degradable polyester replacement (naNea); 99-day marine biodegradation; Cradle to Cradle Gold certified
- **Norsk e-Fuel** (Norway) - Carbon-to-plastics pathway via e-Naphtha; partnership with Braskem for sustainable bioplastics
- **ReLeaf** (Avantium, Netherlands) - Plant-based fossil-free recyclable plastics; part of Avantium portfolio
- **UPM Biofore** (Finland) - Wood-based bioplastics and biocomposites; BioVerno naphtha; vertically integrated

#### Integrated Support Companies
- **FORVIA** (France) - Automotive bioplastics and sustainable materials through MATERI'ACT; NAFILean biocomposites deployed on 9M+ vehicles
- **AIMPLAS** (Spain) - Technology center for bioplastics testing, recycling research, certification; highest ENAC accreditations for plastics in Spain
- **SaniSure** (USA) - Single-use bioprocessing solutions; merging 6 companies; 5 manufacturing facilities globally
- **ExxonMobil** (USA) - Advanced recycling (pyrolysis) and synthetic biology partnerships; Exxtend technology targeting 1M tonnes circular polymers by 2030
- **TotalEnergies** (France) - PLA producer through TotalEnergies Corbion JV; targeting 10% global market share by 2030
- **Gabriel-Chemie** (Austria) - Masterbatch solutions for biopolymers; research in recyclable and sortable bioplastics
- **MiAlgae** - Microalgae-based bioplastics research and development
- **Fraunhofer IPMS** (Germany) - Microplastics alternatives research; cellulose particle development for cosmetics

### Testing & Certification Companies (2 total)

- **European Bioplastics (EUBP)** (Germany) - Industry association managing standards, certification, policy advocacy; Seedling label certification; EN 13432 industrial composability standards
- **NTCP** (Netherlands) - Not-for-profit test center with industrial-scale sorting/washing facilities; design for recycling research; mechanical and chemical recycling testing

### Research Institutes & Universities (2 total)

- **RIKEN Center for Emergent Matter Science (CEMS)** (Japan) - Supramolecular plastics research; ocean-degradable plastic breakthrough; seawater-degradable cellulose variants
- **Northeast Forestry University and Shenyang University of Chemical Technology** (China) - Academic research partnerships in bioplastics and sustainable materials
