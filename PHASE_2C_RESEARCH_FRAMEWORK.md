## Company Identification and Research Methodology

### Step 1: Source Identification and Data Collection

**Primary Sources**:
1. **Industry Associations**:
   - European Bioplastics (EUBP) - Member directory
   - National chemistry/plastics associations
   - Regional bioplastics networks

2. **Databases**:
   - Crunchbase (startups, funding)
   - PitchBook (investments)
   - LinkedIn (company identification)
   - Patent databases (USPTO, WIPO, national offices)

3. **Industry Publications**:
   - Bioplastics Magazine
   - PlasticsNews
   - Interplast magazine
   - International journals

4. **Investment & News Sources**:
   - TechCrunch (startups)
   - VentureBeat (biotech funding)
   - Industry news aggregators
   - Regional business publications

5. **Company Websites & Direct Search**:
   - Web searches for regional producers
   - Government industrial directories
   - Manufacturing capacity announcements
   - Sustainability reports

### Step 2: Initial Screening

**Inclusion Criteria**:
- Produces or plans to produce biopolymers
- Has commercial or near-commercial operations OR clear development plan
- Accessible information available (website, news, publications)
- Geographic location fills identified gaps
- Technology represents underrepresented areas

**Exclusion Criteria**:
- Theoretical/concept stage only (no commercial direction)
- Defunct or ceased operations
- Duplicate entries in existing database
- Equipment manufacturers (unless integrated production)
- Consulting firms without manufacturing

### Step 3: Comprehensive Research

**Data to Compile for Each Company**:
- Company history and founding
- Headquarters and facilities
- Employee count
- Products and biopolymer types
- Production capacity (current and planned)
- Feedstock sources
- Market segments served
- Key partnerships and customers
- Funding history and current status
- Public/private status and stock information
- Certifications and standards
- Recent announcements and growth plans
- Sustainability credentials
- Competitive advantages
- Challenges or risk factors

### Step 4: Categorization and Analysis

**Categorize By**:
- Geographic region
- Primary technology
- Company size and development stage
- Market maturity
- Innovation level
- Production capacity

### Step 5: Priority Ranking

**Priority Score** (1-10):
- Market significance (3 points): Size, regional importance, growth trajectory
- Innovation level (2 points): Novel technology, emerging feedstock, sustainability leadership
- Information availability (2 points): Public information, web presence, accessibility
- Geographic gap fill (2 points): Addresses identified geographic absence
- Technology gap fill (1 point): Represents underrepresented technology

**Select top 60-75 companies for profile creation** based on priority scores
