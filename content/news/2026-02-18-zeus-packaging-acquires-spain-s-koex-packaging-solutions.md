Irish global supply chain management group Zeus Packaging has continued its aggressive international expansion strategy with the acquisition of Koex Packaging Solutions, a specialist manufacturer based in Spain. This move significantly strengthens Zeus’s vertical integration within the European market, particularly in the realm of high-performance flexible packaging and extrusion technologies.

## Strengthening European Extrusion Capabilities

The acquisition of Koex Packaging Solutions provides Zeus with advanced manufacturing infrastructure in the Iberian Peninsula. Koex is renowned for its expertise in co-extrusion technology, a critical process in the production of multi-layer flexible films. By integrating Koex’s assets, Zeus aims to enhance its control over the supply chain for food and industrial packaging, reducing reliance on third-party suppliers and improving speed-to-market for Western European clients.

The deal allows Zeus to leverage Koex’s capabilities in producing down-gauged films. These materials maintain high strength and barrier performance while reducing overall plastic usage, aligning with current EU packaging waste regulations.

## focus on Sustainable Substrates

A primary driver for the acquisition is the potential to scale sustainable material innovation. Koex’s production lines are adaptable to processing next-generation biopolymers. The integration is expected to accelerate the development of compostable co-extruded films and recyclable mono-material structures that offer necessary oxygen and moisture barriers without compromising end-of-life processability.

Zeus has indicated that the Spanish facility will become a hub for developing eco-friendly flexible solutions, specifically targeting the fresh produce and FMCG sectors. This aligns with Zeus’s "Sustainability 2030" roadmap, which focuses on transitioning clients away from virgin fossil-based polymers toward circular and renewable alternatives.

## Continued Global Growth

This transaction marks another milestone in Zeus Packaging's growth trajectory, following a series of strategic acquisitions over the past five years designed to consolidate its position as a global packaging powerhouse. By acquiring manufacturing assets like Koex, rather than solely distribution networks, Zeus is signaling a shift toward becoming a primary producer of technical packaging solutions.
