## Expanding Circular Footprint in Europe

Selenis, a global supplier of premium specialty polyesters, has announced a major strategic investment to double its recycled polyester production capacity at its manufacturing facility in Portalegre, Portugal. Scheduled for full operational completion by the third quarter of 2027, this significant infrastructure expansion is designed to meet the rapidly escalating European and global demand for circular plastic solutions. 

## Advanced Upcycling and Material Performance

The upcoming capacity increase will bolster the production of Selenis’s advanced recycled polyester and copolyester grades. Utilizing sophisticated recycling technologies, the Portuguese facility upcycles post-consumer PET (polyethylene terephthalate) waste into high-performance, food-safe resins. These sustainable materials are engineered to match the precise technical and aesthetic properties of virgin fossil-based plastics. 

For packaging converters and consumer brand owners, this expansion guarantees greater access to recycled materials that do not compromise on essential packaging characteristics, such as high optical clarity, chemical resistance, and robust mechanical strength. Selenis’s capability to handle complex polymer architectures ensures that even hard-to-recycle PET streams can be successfully reintegrated into high-value applications, driving true closed-loop circularity.

## Strategic Growth Amidst Regulatory Pressure

This production scale-up arrives at a critical juncture for the European packaging sector. With stringent EU frameworks, such as the Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation (PPWR), mandating ambitious recycled content targets by 2030, a reliable supply of high-quality rPET is becoming essential. By doubling its output in Portugal, Selenis is positioning itself as a pivotal supply chain partner for the cosmetics, personal care, healthcare, and food and beverage industries.

Furthermore, expanding localized production in Portugal enhances supply chain resilience for European markets. It minimizes the logistical carbon footprint associated with material transport and ensures a steady, sustainable flow of specialized resins to regional manufacturers.
