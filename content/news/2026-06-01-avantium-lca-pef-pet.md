**AMSTERDAM, Netherlands** — **Avantium N.V.**, a leader in renewable and circular polymer materials, has published an **updated Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)** for its **releaf® polyethylene furanoate (PEF)**, confirming that the plant-based polymer can reduce greenhouse gas emissions by **up to 88%** in 500 mL beverage bottle applications compared to conventional PET, under representative European conditions.

The study, conducted in accordance with **ISO 14040/14044** standards and critically reviewed by an independent panel, represents the most comprehensive environmental assessment of PEF to date, incorporating **primary production data** from Avantium's flagship **FDCA plant in Delfzijl, Netherlands** (operational since 2024).

## Key LCA Findings

| Impact Category | PEF (releaf®) vs PET | Basis |
|-----------------|---------------------|-------|
| **Climate Change (GWP100)** | **Up to -88%** | 500 mL bottle, EU electricity mix |
| **Fossil Resource Scarcity** | **-85%** | Avoided petroleum feedstock |
| **Water Consumption** | **-45%** | Lower process water intensity |
| **Land Use** | **+12%** | Agricultural feedstock (sugar beet/wheat) |

**Functional unit**: 500 mL carbonated beverage bottle, including closure and label, produced and filled in Europe, with end-of-life via existing PET recycling stream.

## Feedstock & Process Innovations

The updated LCA reflects several improvements since the 2019 assessment:

1. **Commercial-scale FDCA data** from Delfzijl plant (vs. pilot data previously)
2. **Optimized YXY® process** with improved catalyst lifetime and yield
3. **Renewable electricity** procurement for Dutch operations (100% wind/solar)
4. **Second-generation feedstock readiness**: wheat straw, corn stover pathways modeled
5. **PET/PEF multilayer compatibility** validated by RecyClass for mechanical recycling

> "This LCA confirms that releaf® delivers on its core promise: a **drop-in, high-performance polyester** that drastically reduces carbon emissions while fitting into existing recycling infrastructure," said **Tom van Aken, CEO of Avantium**. "With the Delfzijl plant operational and licensing discussions advancing, we have the data foundation to scale."

## Recyclability & Circularity

Critical for market adoption: **PEF is fully compatible with PET recycling streams** at up to **5-10% blend ratios** without quality degradation, per **RecyClass** and **EPBP** testing. Avantium's PET/PEF multilayer bottle (PEF barrier layer, PET structural layer) has received **RecyClass "Class A" recyclability approval** — enabling monolayer-like barrier performance in a recyclable structure.

## Market Context

- **PEF properties**: Superior O₂/CO₂ barrier (10x/4x PET), higher mechanical strength, 12°C higher Tg
- **Target applications**: Carbonated soft drinks, beer, juices, dairy, hot-fill
- **Commercial strategy**: Technology licensing + FDCA supply from Delfzijl (50 kt/year nameplate)
- **Partnerships**: Coca-Cola, Danone, Carlsberg, ALPLA, Toyobo, Mitsui, Hünersdorfer

## Regulatory Alignment

The LCA supports compliance with:
- **EU Packaging & Packaging Waste Regulation (PPWR)**: recycled content & recyclability targets
- **Product Environmental Footprint (PEF) method**: standardized LCA for green claims
- **French AGEC Law / Italian Green Claims Decree**: substantiation requirements
