## Kingfa Showcases Full-Chain Carbon Reduction Pathway at COP30

At COP30 in November 2025, **Academician Li Jianjun** of Kingfa Sci. & Tech. presented the company's comprehensive green low-carbon advanced material solutions, contributing to the sustainable development of global industrial chains.

The presentation focused on Kingfa's **full-chain carbon reduction pathway** for the plastics and materials industry, demonstrating how integrated material solutions can enable decarbonisation across manufacturing sectors.

## Three-Pillar Carbon Strategy

Kingfa's carbon neutrality approach rests on three interconnected pillars:

### 1. Bio-Based Feedstock Development
Replacing petrochemical feedstocks with bio-based alternatives through green biomanufacturing. Kingfa's ECOPOND range of biodegradable compounds (PLA, PBAT, PBS, PGA) demonstrates the commercial viability of bio-based materials across packaging, agriculture, and consumer applications.

### 2. Recycled Content and Circular Economy
Expanding both mechanical and chemical recycling capacity to create closed-loop material flows. Kingfa targets **1 million tonnes/year** of total recycling capacity globally, with flagship operations including the Montalbo chemical recycling facility in Spain (30,000 t/yr).

### 3. Lifecycle Carbon Footprint Calculation
Providing internationally certified low-carbon materials with end-to-end solutions covering recycling, product design, production, and traceability. Kingfa offers **lifecycle carbon footprint calculation services** to help value chain partners quantify and reduce their Scope 3 emissions.

## China's Carbon Neutrality Context

China has set ambitious targets: **carbon peaking by 2030 and carbon neutrality by 2060**. Biomanufacturing is emerging as a key solution in this transition — bio-based materials are eco-friendly and sustainable, produced through biochemical technologies with biocatalysis that can significantly reduce the carbon intensity of materials compared to petrochemical routes.

Kingfa's presentation at COP30 positioned the company as a bridge between China's national climate ambitions and the practical material solutions needed by global manufacturers to meet their own decarbonisation commitments.

## Industry Recognition

Kingfa's sustainability leadership has been recognised internationally. In December 2023, the company received the **"Enterprise of the Year" Award** on the Bloomberg Green ESG50 Outstanding List — the highest honour bestowed by the programme — standing out among hundreds of participating companies.

## About Kingfa

Kingfa Sci. & Tech. Co., Ltd. (SSE: 600143) is Asia's largest modified plastics company with 64 subsidiaries worldwide. The company's ECOPOND biodegradable compounds and expanding recycling operations position it at the intersection of China's plastics industry and global sustainability imperatives.
