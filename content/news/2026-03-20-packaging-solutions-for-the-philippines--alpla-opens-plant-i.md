## Expanding Sustainable Packaging in Southeast Asia

ALPLA, a global leader in packaging solutions, has officially opened a state-of-the-art manufacturing plant in Calamba City, Philippines. Marking a significant expansion of its operational footprint in the Asia-Pacific region, the new facility is purpose-built to meet the surging regional demand for advanced, sustainable packaging formats, including bio-based plastics and highly recyclable polymer structures.

## Driving Bioplastic Integration and Circularity

The Calamba City facility is equipped with cutting-edge injection moulding and extrusion blow moulding technologies that have been specifically optimized for processing next-generation materials. Processing biopolymers requires highly precise thermal management to prevent material degradation and to maintain optimal melt flow indices during production. ALPLA’s newly installed machinery is engineered to seamlessly handle both mechanically recycled polymers and advanced bioplastics, including bio-PET and polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHA) blends. 

As consumer goods brands increasingly pivot away from conventional fossil-based plastics, ALPLA’s new plant ensures that bioplastic alternatives can be scaled for commercial packaging lines without compromising on critical barrier properties or structural integrity. By localizing the production of these eco-friendly materials, ALPLA significantly reduces the carbon footprint associated with global supply chain logistics.

## Empowering the Philippine Market

This strategic investment fortifies ALPLA's commitment to a closed-loop circular economy while bringing vital economic growth to the Laguna province. The facility operates under stringent environmental standards, prioritizing energy-efficient manufacturing and zero-waste production principles. 

By introducing advanced bioplastic processing capabilities directly to the local market, ALPLA is actively positioning the Philippines as a crucial hub for sustainable packaging. This localization enables local and multinational consumer brands to effectively meet newly implemented Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) regulations and their own corporate environmental mandates. The Calamba City plant represents a critical step forward in reducing the region's reliance on virgin fossil plastics across the robust Southeast Asian consumer market.
