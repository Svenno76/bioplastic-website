**AMSTERDAM, Netherlands** — **Avantium N.V.** and **Heynen Systems B.V.**, a Dutch technical textile knitting company and interliner automation specialist, have announced a collaboration to develop and assess applications of **releaf® polyethylene furanoate (PEF)** in the **bedding and upholstery industry**, with a focus on **comfort foam and technical textile** applications.

The partnership aims to validate PEF's performance in fiber and foam formulations for mattress components, pillow cores, and upholstery padding — markets where sustainability demands are rising but technical requirements for durability, comfort, and fire safety remain stringent.

## Collaboration Scope

| Partner | Contribution |
|---------|--------------|
| **Avantium** | Supplies releaf® PEF polymer (100% plant-based, recyclable polyester) and YXY® process technology |
| **Heynen Systems** | Technical textile development: knitting, interlining, foam lamination, automation integration |

**Target applications**:
- **Comfort foam** formulations incorporating PEF-based polyols
- **Technical knitted textiles** for mattress ticking and upholstery covers
- **Interliner automation** for efficient production of PEF-enhanced bedding components

## Why PEF for Bedding & Upholstery?

**releaf® PEF** offers a unique property profile for comfort applications:

| Property | Advantage for Bedding/Upholstery |
|----------|----------------------------------|
| **High Tg (~86°C)** | Thermal stability in foam processing & use |
| **Excellent mechanical strength** | Durability in high-load applications (mattresses) |
| **Inherent flame retardancy potential** | Reduced additive load for fire standards (EN 597, CAL 117) |
| **Bio-based content (100%)** | Meets growing specifier demand for renewable materials |
| **Recyclable in polyester streams** | End-of-life pathway via existing textile recycling |

## Market Context

The European bedding and upholstery sector faces converging pressures:
- **EU Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR)**: digital product passports, recycled content targets
- **Furniture & bedding brands** (IKEA, Tempur, Hilding Anders, Recticel) setting public circularity goals
- **Consumer demand**: "plant-based" and "plastic-free" claims gaining traction in home textiles
- **Foam industry transition**: move away from TDI/MDI-based PU foams toward bio-based polyols

PEF's **polyester chemistry** enables compatibility with existing **PET/PES recycling infrastructure** — a critical advantage over novel bio-based chemistries lacking collection streams.

## Demonstration Timeline

- **Interzum Forum Italy (Bergamo, June 4–5, 2026)**: First PEF-based comfort foam and textile prototypes displayed
- **H2 2026**: Pilot production runs for mattress component testing
- **2027 target**: Commercial sampling to bedding OEMs

## Company Boilerplates

**Avantium N.V.** (Euronext Amsterdam: AVTX) develops YXY® technology for FDCA and PEF — a 100% plant-based, recyclable polyester with superior barrier and mechanical properties. The Delfzijl, Netherlands plant is the world's first commercial FDCA facility.
