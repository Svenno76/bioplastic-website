Inteplast Group, a global leader in the manufacturing of integrated plastics and films, has officially announced the acquisition of STA, a specialized manufacturer known for its high-performance sustainable over-laminates and coating technologies. The strategic move, finalized on February 12, 2026, marks a significant step in Inteplast's efforts to vertically integrate advanced surface technologies with its extensive bioplastic and polyolefin film capabilities.

## Strengthening the Value Chain

The acquisition incorporates STA’s assets, intellectual property, and manufacturing lines into Inteplast’s World-Pak division. STA has established a reputation for developing coatings that do not inhibit the recyclability or compostability of the substrate materials. By bringing STA under the Inteplast umbrella, the group aims to streamline the supply chain for converters and brand owners looking for "all-in-one" sustainable packaging solutions that require sophisticated surface treatments for print protection and aesthetic enhancement.

Key to this acquisition is STA’s proprietary technology in water-based and solvent-free adhesive coatings, which align with current regulatory pressures to reduce Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs) in packaging production.

## Enhancing Bioplastic Performance

For the bioplastics sector, the integration of STA’s technology addresses a critical technical hurdle: barrier performance and durability. Biopolymers often require specialized over-laminates to maintain structural integrity and moisture resistance without compromising end-of-life scenarios.

STA’s portfolio includes bio-based coating formulations compatible with PLA and PHA substrates. These coatings are engineered to degrade at the same rate as the base compostable film, ensuring that the final package meets ASTM D6400 standards for industrial composting. This synergy allows Inteplast to offer films that are not only bio-based but also feature the scuff resistance and optical clarity previously reserved for conventional petroleum-based laminates.

## Strategic Outlook

Inteplast Group leadership emphasized that the acquisition is driven by a market shift toward functional sustainability. By owning the coating technology, Inteplast can now innovate faster, creating barrier films that are thinner yet more effective, reducing overall material usage while enhancing the shelf-life of perishable goods. The company plans to scale STA’s existing operations to meet the growing global demand for premium, eco-conscious flexible packaging.
