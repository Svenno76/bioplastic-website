## A New Era in Flexible Packaging

Canadian plastics manufacturers PolyExpert and Polykar Industries have officially announced the completion of a "merger of equals," resulting in the creation of a newly unified corporate entity: Invera Flexibles. This strategic consolidation establishes one of North America's most robust players in the sustainable flexible packaging and compostable film sectors.

The merger unites two highly complementary forces. Polykar Industries has long been recognized for its pioneering work in manufacturing industrial and commercial compostable bags, alongside a well-established plastics recycling division. PolyExpert brings decades of specialized technical expertise in advanced polyethylene film extrusion. By combining their operations, Invera Flexibles instantly gains enhanced manufacturing scale, a much broader product portfolio, and expanded technical capabilities.

## Scaling Compostable Packaging Solutions

From a material science perspective, the formation of Invera Flexibles arrives at a critical juncture for the bioplastics industry. Polykar's established proficiency in processing biodegradable polymers—specifically PBAT (polybutylene adipate terephthalate) and PLA (polylactic acid) blends—will now directly benefit from PolyExpert’s extensive extrusion infrastructure. 

This synergy is expected to rapidly accelerate the development and commercialization of certified compostable packaging solutions. As municipalities and retailers across North America expand organic waste diversion programs, the demand for high-performance compostable liners and flexible bioplastic films has surged. Invera Flexibles is uniquely positioned to supply these complex biopolymer films at high volumes without compromising on mechanical strength, sealability, or barrier properties.

## Strategic Expansion and R&D Focus

Beyond immediate production capabilities, the merger signals a strong operational commitment to sustainable research and development. The newly integrated company plans to leverage its combined technological assets to engineer the next generation of flexible films. By harmonizing Polykar's recycling operations with PolyExpert's film production, Invera Flexibles also enhances closed-loop manufacturing processes, reducing the overall carbon footprint of their product lines. Moving forward, the company aims to help consumer packaged goods (CPG) brands meet increasingly stringent environmental regulations through novel bioplastic formulations.
