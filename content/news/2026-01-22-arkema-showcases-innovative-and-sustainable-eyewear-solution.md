## Arkema Brings Bio-Based Performance to MIDO 2026

Global specialty materials leader Arkema is set to unveil its latest portfolio of high-performance, renewable polymer solutions at the MIDO 2026 international eyewear exhibition in Milan. As consumer demand for eco-conscious fashion and activewear accelerates, Arkema is positioning its advanced bioplastic technologies as the premium standard for the future of optical frames and sports lenses.

## Advancing Eyewear with Castor-Based Polymers

At the core of Arkema’s showcase is its renowned family of bio-based polymers, which are exclusively derived from renewable castor beans—a drought-resistant crop that does not compete with the human food chain. The company is highlighting its latest material grades within the Rilsan® Polyamide 11 (PA11) and Pebax® Rnew® elastomer ranges. 

Unlike traditional petroleum-based nylons or polycarbonates, these advanced bio-circular materials offer a drastically reduced carbon footprint. Furthermore, they achieve this environmental benefit without compromising the stringent mechanical properties required by luxury and athletic eyewear brands. Rilsan® PA11 is heavily utilized in the optical industry for its exceptional lightness, chemical resistance, and dynamic flexibility, making it an ideal solution for durable, lightweight frames. Concurrently, Pebax® Rnew® elastomers provide unmatched energy return and impact resistance, properties that are highly sought after in the demanding sports optics segment.

## Merging Design Freedom with Circularity

Beyond raw material performance, Arkema’s presence at MIDO 2026 underscores a shifting paradigm in eyewear manufacturing. Industrial designers are increasingly searching for resins that enable complex aesthetic geometries while supporting circular economy principles. Arkema’s transparent and opaque bioplastic solutions boast high bio-based carbon content and integrate seamlessly into existing injection molding infrastructure. 

By supplying easily processable, renewable alternatives, Arkema empowers global eyewear brands to transition smoothly away from fossil-based plastics. The MIDO exhibition serves as a critical platform for the company to connect with designers and compounders, reinforcing its technical leadership in driving material transitions across the consumer goods sector.
