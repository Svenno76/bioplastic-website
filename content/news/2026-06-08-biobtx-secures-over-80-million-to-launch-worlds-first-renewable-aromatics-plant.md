In a landmark investment for the circular chemistry industry, BioBTX, a pioneering developer of renewable aromatics technology based in Groningen, Netherlands, has secured over €80 million to launch its first commercial-scale plant.

### Converting Plastic Waste into High-Value Chemicals

The new facility at the PETRA Circular Chemicals Plant in Delfzijl will process 20,000 tons of mixed plastic waste annually using BioBTX's innovative ICCP (Integrated Cascaded Catalytic Pyrolysis) technology. The plant will produce renewable aromatics (BTX — benzene, toluene, and xylene) — essential chemical building blocks used in insulating foams, coatings, PET bottles, batteries, and pharmaceuticals.

### Replacing Fossil Feedstocks

Traditionally, aromatics are derived from crude oil through energy-intensive refining processes. BioBTX's technology offers a sustainable alternative by converting low-value plastic waste that would otherwise be incinerated or landfilled into the same high-value chemical products. This represents a significant carbon reduction compared to conventional fossil-based aromatic production.

### Scaling Circular Chemistry

Since its founding in 2012, BioBTX has been developing and de-risking its technology at pilot scale. The €80 million investment now enables the transition to commercial production. Once proven at scale, the company intends to license its technology globally, enabling the chemical industry to systematically replace fossil feedstocks with circular alternatives sourced from plastic waste.
