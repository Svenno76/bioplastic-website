Austrian packaging specialist **ALPLA** and independent technology institute **NTCP** have launched a groundbreaking four-year pilot project in Heerenveen, Netherlands, to develop the first certified process for producing food-grade recycled high-density polyethylene (HDPE). Funded by the Dutch Ministry of Climate Policy and Green Growth, the initiative employs a patented solvent-based technology to clean and process post-consumer HDPE waste, positioning the companies to meet upcoming EU regulatory requirements.

## Background

The pilot plant began operations following successful laboratory phases, with intensive testing of all process steps now underway. ALPLA has established a dedicated recycling company in the Netherlands to support both the demonstration phase and potential commercial deployment. The solvent-based process represents a significant technological advancement, as no certified EU process currently exists for producing food-grade recycled HDPE material. Michael Heyde, ALPLA's recycling division technology head, described the innovation as "a real game changer" for the industry.

## Significance

The project directly addresses the EU's **Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation (PPWR)**, which mandates minimum recycled content in packaging from 2030 onward. Success would enable ALPLA to supply high-quality recycled HDPE for food packaging in the fast-moving consumer goods sector at industrial scale.
