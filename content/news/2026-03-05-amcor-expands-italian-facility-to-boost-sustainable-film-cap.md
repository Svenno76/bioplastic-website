## Scaling Up Sustainable Packaging

Global packaging giant Amcor has officially announced a significant expansion of its manufacturing facility in Italy, a move designed to drastically increase the production capacity of its sustainable film portfolio. The strategic investment will enable the plant to scale up the manufacturing of innovative flexible packaging solutions, specifically focusing on materials engineered to be fully recyclable, reusable, or compostable.

This expansion responds to a rapidly accelerating demand from consumer goods brands seeking to transition away from conventional, hard-to-recycle multi-material plastics. By enhancing its European infrastructure, Amcor aims to provide localized, high-volume access to advanced packaging films that deliver premium barrier protection and product shelf-life without sacrificing environmental end-of-life options.

## Advancing Material Technologies

The Italian plant's upgraded extrusion and converting lines will focus on specialized polymer structures, including mono-material polyethylene (PE) films and certified compostable bioplastic alternatives. Producing high-performance compostable films requires complex polymer engineering to ensure the material provides stringent oxygen and moisture barriers during its use phase, while efficiently breaking down under industrial composting conditions to meet rigorous European standards. 

This capacity boost directly supports Amcor’s broader corporate sustainability pledge to make 100% of its packaging recyclable, reusable, or compostable. By integrating state-of-the-art production technology, the facility will also operate with improved energy efficiency, lowering the overall carbon footprint of the manufactured films.

## Driving the Circular Economy in Europe

Amcor's strategic expansion in Italy arrives at a crucial juncture for the European packaging sector, which is navigating the strict mandates of the impending Packaging and Packaging Waste Regulation (PPWR). By localizing the production of these advanced bioplastics and circular materials, Amcor is strongly positioning itself to help European fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG) brands meet aggressive regulatory targets and fulfill consumer expectations for environmental responsibility.
