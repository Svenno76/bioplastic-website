## A Milestone for the Circular Economy in France

TotalEnergies has officially commenced production at France’s first advanced plastics recycling plant, marking a transformative step forward for the nation's circular economy infrastructure. This landmark facility is specifically engineered to tackle the growing environmental challenge of end-of-life plastics that traditional mechanical recycling systems simply cannot process. 

## Leveraging Advanced Pyrolysis Technology

At the core of the new plant's operations is cutting-edge advanced recycling technology, commonly referred to as chemical recycling. The facility utilizes a sophisticated pyrolysis process to break down complex, mixed, and heavily contaminated plastic waste streams. By heating these hard-to-recycle plastics at high temperatures in the absence of oxygen, the plant efficiently converts the waste into a high-quality liquid feedstock known as pyrolysis oil.

This valuable recycled feedstock is then seamlessly integrated into existing petrochemical facilities to manufacture virgin-quality polymers. Crucially, the resulting materials exhibit physical and chemical properties identical to traditional fossil-based plastics. This makes them perfectly suited for high-demand, highly regulated applications—such as food-contact packaging and sensitive medical devices—where mechanically recycled plastics typically fail to meet strict safety and performance standards.

## Driving Corporate and Regional Sustainability Goals

The successful start-up of this facility aligns perfectly with TotalEnergies' ambitious strategic objective to produce 30% circular polymers by the year 2030. By heavily investing in advanced recycling capacity, the energy giant is actively bridging the gap between municipal waste management and industrial petrochemical production. This provides global consumer goods brands with a reliable, scalable pathway to meet their increasingly stringent recycled content targets.

Furthermore, this new French plant will divert thousands of tons of plastic waste away from regional incinerators and landfills while simultaneously reducing the industry's heavy reliance on virgin fossil feedstocks. As the regulatory landscape across Europe continues to demand higher recycling rates, TotalEnergies’ latest operational milestone establishes a critical blueprint for the future of sustainable polymer manufacturing.
