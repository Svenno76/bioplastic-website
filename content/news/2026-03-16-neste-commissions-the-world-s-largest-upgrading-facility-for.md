## A Historic Milestone in Chemical Recycling

Finnish refining giant Neste has officially commissioned the world's largest upgrading facility for liquefied waste plastic at its industrial hub in Porvoo. Unveiled on March 16, 2026, the plant represents a monumental step forward in the commercialization of chemical recycling, providing the vital infrastructure necessary to process previously unrecyclable plastic waste on an unprecedented industrial scale.

## Transforming Waste into Virgin-Quality Feedstock

The new facility is specifically engineered to tackle a major bottleneck in the advanced recycling supply chain: the high impurity levels typically found in raw liquefied waste plastic, commonly known as pyrolysis oil. While thermal liquefaction successfully breaks down hard-to-recycle mixed and multi-layer plastics, the resulting raw oil requires rigorous purification before it can be integrated into standard petrochemical operations.

Neste's cutting-edge upgrading unit refines this raw, impure pyrolysis oil into a premium, drop-in feedstock. This high-quality output can directly replace virgin fossil oil in the steam cracking process to produce new plastics and chemicals. Crucially, the resulting polymers offer the exact same safety, durability, and performance characteristics as fossil-based equivalents. This makes the feedstock ideal for heavily regulated applications, such as food-contact packaging and medical devices, where mechanically recycled materials are often prohibited.

## Scaling the Circular Economy

By operating at this newly commissioned capacity, Neste is effectively bridging the critical gap between pilot-phase recycling technologies and commercial-level polymer production. The facility will significantly increase the volume of end-of-life plastic that can be diverted from incineration and landfills, closing the loop on complex polymer waste.

This strategic scale-up underscores Neste’s long-term commitment to reducing fossil dependence in polymer production. As global regulatory pressures and consumer demand for high-quality recycled plastics surge, this upgrading facility establishes a robust, operational blueprint for advancing the circular economy.
