Michigan State University scientists have achieved a breakthrough in sustainable materials production by creating biodegradable bioplastics using nothing more than sunlight and bacteria.

### How It Works

The research team has engineered bacteria that can convert sunlight and carbon dioxide directly into bioplastic materials. This carbon-negative manufacturing process represents a paradigm shift in how plastics could be produced — removing CO₂ from the atmosphere while creating useful materials.

### Growing Expectations for Algae and Seaweed

The breakthrough is part of a broader trend toward algae and seaweed-based solutions in the bio-economy. These organisms offer a sustainable feedstock that doesn't compete with food production and can be cultivated using minimal resources.

The research opens new possibilities for scaling bioplastic production in a way that is both economically viable and environmentally beneficial.
