## Overview

Carbon dioxide captured from industrial emissions or direct air capture (DAC) used as carbon source for chemicals, fuels, and materials via electrochemical, catalytic, or biological pathways. Enables carbon-negative materials if paired with renewable energy. Key pathways: CO₂ → methanol → olefins; CO₂ → formic acid; CO₂ → polycarbonates; microbial electrosynthesis.

## Key Concepts

CO₂ as feedstock turns waste carbon into valuable products — carbon-negative if renewable energy powers the conversion.
