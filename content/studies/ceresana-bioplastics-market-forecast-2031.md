## Executive Summary

The global bioplastics market is witnessing significant growth as industries and consumers increasingly shift toward sustainable materials. According to Ceresana's latest market report, the market is projected to reach **$18.7 billion by 2031**, with the biodegradable plastics segment showing the strongest growth trajectory.

## Key Findings

### Market Drivers

- **Regulatory pressure**: The EU Single-Use Plastics Directive and Extended Producer Responsibility requirements are accelerating adoption of bioplastics across packaging applications.
- **Corporate sustainability commitments**: Major CPG companies have pledged to transition to bio-based and biodegradable materials.
- **Consumer demand**: Growing awareness of plastic pollution is driving consumer preference for eco-friendly alternatives.

### Regional Trends

**Europe** continues to lead in bioplastics adoption, supported by the European Green Deal and the Single-Use Plastics Directive. **Asia-Pacific** is the fastest-growing region, driven by large-scale production capacity expansions in China and Southeast Asia.

### Segment Analysis

The biodegradable plastics segment — including PLA, PHA, and starch-based materials — is expected to outpace conventional bio-based plastics in growth rate, driven by end-of-life advantages and regulatory incentives.

## Outlook

The report forecasts that bioplastics will continue to move from niche applications to mainstream adoption across packaging, agriculture, automotive, and consumer goods sectors through 2031.
