## Overview

Kaneka Corporation is a major Japanese chemical and materials company headquartered in Tokyo and founded in 1949. Listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange (TSE:4118), Kaneka operates globally with over 1,000 employees and diversified chemical, functional plastics, and bioplastics operations. The company pioneered the industrialization of PHBH (polyhydroxybutyrate-co-hydroxyhexanoate) biopolymers, achieving the remarkable capability of seawater biodegradationâ€”a distinguishing feature that addresses global concerns about marine plastic pollution.

Kaneka's bioplastics division produces Green Planet brand PHA materials, representing a breakthrough achievement in marine-compatible bioplastics. With production at its Takasago facility in Japan and recent capacity expansion to 20,000 MT/year (completed January 2024), Kaneka has positioned itself as a global leader in seawater-degradable biopolymers.

### PHBH Technology - Green Planet Brand

**PHBH Copolymer Specialization**: Kaneka pioneered the industrialization of PHBH, achieving technical and commercial success where others had failed:
- First company to successfully industrialize PHBH copolymer at commercial scale
- Takasago facility operational since 2011 with consistent production growth
- Expanded capacity from initial levels to 20,000 MT/year by January 2024

**Seawater Biodegradation - Unique Achievement**: Kaneka's PHBH possesses the remarkable capability to biodegrade in seawater and marine environments:
- Addresses global ocean plastic pollution crisis
- Certified seawater biodegradability through rigorous testing
- Material degrades to natural components (water, CO2, biomass)
- No microplastic residues or persistent polymers
- Distinct from PHA that require composting facilities

**Green Planet Brand Portfolio**:

**Polymeric Properties**:
