## Overview

Kingfa Sci. & Tech. Co., Ltd. (金发科技) is Asia's largest and first stock-market-listed company in the modified plastics industry. Founded in 1993 and headquartered in Guangzhou Science City, the company operates 64 subsidiaries worldwide and has grown into a globally preeminent supplier of advanced materials.

Through its dedicated subsidiary **Zhuhai Kingfa Biomaterial Co., Ltd.**, Kingfa produces a comprehensive range of biodegradable and compostable bioplastic compounds under the **ECOPOND** brand. These materials serve the rapidly growing demand for sustainable packaging, agricultural films, and single-use alternatives in both domestic Chinese and international markets.

With revenue exceeding RMB 63 billion in 2025 and a workforce of over 10,000, Kingfa combines the scale of a major industrial conglomerate with deep expertise in polymer science and green chemistry.

### Key Brands and Product Lines

**ECOPOND® Biodegradable Compounds**: The flagship bioplastics brand encompassing:
- **ECOPOND Flex** — PBAT-based flexible film compounds for compostable bags, agricultural mulch films, and food packaging
- **ECOPOND Rigid** — PLA/PBS-based rigid compounds for cutlery, containers, thermoformed trays, and 3D printing filaments
- **ECOPOND Coating** — PBS-based extrusion coating materials for paper cups and barrier packaging (home-compostable)

**Specialty Biopolymers**: PLA, PBAT, PBS, and PGA resins for compounding and direct processing.

**Modified Plastics**: The company's traditional strength — engineering plastics, high-temperature nylons, LCP, and polyethersulfone for automotive, electronics, and construction.

## Production and Facilities

Kingfa's bioplastics production is centered at its **Zhuhai Biomaterial Base** (53.3 hectares / 800 mu), one of the largest dedicated bioplastics manufacturing facilities in Asia. The site houses:

- Biodegradable plastics production lines
- High-temperature nylon and specialty polymer facilities
- R&D laboratories for polymer modification and formulation development
- Compounding and pelletisation equipment

## Certifications and Standards

Kingfa's bioplastic products hold the following certifications:
- **EN 13432** — Industrial compostability
- **ASTM D6400** — US compostability standard
- **OK Compost / OK Compost Home** (TÜV Austria) — Industrial and home compostability
- **BPI Certified Compostable** — Biodegradable Products Institute (US)
- **GB/T 28206** — Chinese biodegradable plastics standard

## Sustainability Strategy

Kingfa has positioned sustainability as a core strategic pillar, aligning with China's national goals of carbon peaking by 2030 and carbon neutrality by 2060.

### Green Low-Carbon Material Solutions

At COP30 (November 2025), Kingfa's Academician Li Jianjun presented the company's **full-chain carbon reduction pathway**, covering:
- Bio-based feedstock development and green biomanufacturing
- Recycled content and circular economy integration
- Lifecycle carbon footprint calculation services for value chain partners
- End-to-end solutions from recycling through product design, production, and traceability

### Recycling Expansion

Kingfa aims to achieve **1 million tonnes/year** of plastic recycling capacity, with facilities in China and Europe. The Montalbo, Spain facility (inaugurated February 2025) is the company's flagship European recycling operation with 30,000 tonnes/year capacity for high-performance recycled plastics.

### Strategic Partnerships

In early 2026, Kingfa signed a strategic cooperation agreement with **Beijing University of Chemical Technology** for green biomanufacturing — targeting bio-based material development using biochemical technologies and biocatalysis to replace petrochemical feedstocks.

## Market Position

Kingfa is the dominant bioplastics player in the Chinese market, which is the world's largest consumer of biodegradable plastics driven by:
- Provincial single-use plastic bans (Jilin, Hainan, and others since 2015)
- National plastic pollution control policies
- Growing demand for certified compostable alternatives

Globally, Kingfa competes with BASF (Ecoflex), Novamont (Mater-Bi), and NatureWorks (Ingeo PLA), but offers uniquely vertically integrated capabilities spanning resin production, compounding, and recycling.
