## Overview

Mitsubishi Chemical is a major Japanese multinational chemical company headquartered in Tokyo and founded in 1933, with over 90 years of chemical industry expertise. Publicly listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange (TSE:4188) with 1,000+ employees, Mitsubishi Chemical has diversified operations spanning petrochemicals, specialty chemicals, performance polymers, and advanced bioplastics. The company holds position as world's No. 2 copolyester producer globally, competing directly with SK Chemicals for market leadership in biodegradable polyester production.

Mitsubishi Chemical's bioplastics division focuses on developing and commercializing advanced biodegradable polymers that address global demand for sustainable materials. The company's portfolio includes BioPBS (polybutylene succinate) and DURABIO (bio-based engineering plastic), representing cutting-edge innovations in bio-based and biodegradable polymer chemistry. With global manufacturing capabilities and established relationships with major industrial customers, Mitsubishi Chemical leverages its scale and chemical expertise to advance bioplastics commercialization.

### DURABIO Technology - Bio-Based Engineering Plastic

**DURABIO Innovation**: Mitsubishi Chemical's DURABIO represents breakthrough innovation in bio-based engineering plastics:
- Bio-based polyester derived from plant-derived isosorbide
- Completely transparent and colorless
- High heat resistance and thermal stability
- Superior impact resistance
- BPA-free formulation
- Renewable feedstock (100% bio-derived in some grades)

**Isosorbide Derivation**: DURABIO utilizes isosorbide, a diol molecule derived from glucose and other plant sugars:
- Fermentation converts plant sugars to glucose
- Chemical synthesis produces isosorbide monomer
- Polymerization creates rigid transparent polymer
- Enhanced properties vs conventional bio-based plastics
- Enables applications previously limited to petroleum plastics
