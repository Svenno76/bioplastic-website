## Overview

Ecopha Biotech is an Australian biotechnology company pioneering eco-friendly polyhydroxyalkanoate (PHA) bioplastics derived from renewable resources. Founded in 2008 in Malaysia and later expanding to Australia in 2013, Ecopha has become a leading innovator in fully biodegradable biopolymers for food packaging, agriculture, medical, and textile applications.

The company is known for breakthrough innovations including the world-first production of PHB from pongamia oil—a carbon-sequestering, non-edible crop—enabling scalable production of marine-safe, microplastic-free bioplastics.

### Core Technology: PHA Biopolymers

**What is PHA:**
- Polyhydroxyalkanoates—fully biodegradable biopolymers
- Synthesized from renewable resources (sugars, vegetable oils)
- Biodegrades in soil and marine environments without microplastic residue
- No industrial composting required

**Manufacturing Feedstocks:**
- Sugar-based fermentation
- Vegetable oils
- **Pongamia Oil (Innovation):** Carbon-sequestering, non-edible crop oil enabling sustainable, large-scale PHA production

### Breakthrough Innovation: Pongamia-Based PHB

**World First Achievement:**
- Developed novel method to produce PHB (a type of PHA) from pongamia oil
- **Benefits:**
  - Uses non-food crop (avoids food security concerns)
  - Carbon-negative production (pongamia sequesters carbon)
  - Scalable to industrial production levels
  - Produces marine-biodegradable, microplastic-free plastic
  - PCT patent-filed technology
  - FDA and food-grade certified

**Environmental Impact:**
- Eliminates microplastic pollution in oceans
- Supports circular economy in marine environments
- Zero reliance on industrial composting infrastructure
