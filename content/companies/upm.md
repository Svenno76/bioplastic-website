## Overview

UPM (United Paper Mills) is a Helsinki-headquartered multinational forest industry company that has transformed from a traditional pulp and paper producer into a leader in renewable material solutions. With over 17,000 employees and operations in more than 40 countries, UPM develops sustainable alternatives to fossil-based materials across six business areas: UPM Fibres, UPM Energy, UPM Raflatac, UPM Specialty Papers, UPM Plywood, and UPM Biocomposites.

### Bioplastics and Bio-based Materials

UPM's bioplastics activities span multiple business units:

#### UPM Biocomposites
UPM Biocomposites develops, manufactures, and sells high-quality composite products for outdoor construction, consumer applications, and industrial uses. The flagship product line, **EcoAce**, is a wood-based biocomposite that combines wood fibers with renewable biopolymers. These composites reduce material consumption through enhanced durability and are manufactured using renewable feedstocks from UPM's own pulp production.

#### UPM BioVerno
UPM BioVerno is a renewable naphtha produced from crude tall oil, a residue of pulp production. This bio-based feedstock has been successfully converted into **bio-based polyethylene** through partnerships with Dow and Elopak. The resulting bioplastic maintains identical properties to fossil-based PE but with a significantly lower carbon footprint.

#### Partnership with SABIC
In collaboration with SABIC, UPM has developed new wood-based biocomposite materials using certified renewable PP. These materials combine UPM's sustainable wood fiber expertise with SABIC's polymer technology, creating materials suitable for packaging, automotive, and consumer goods applications.

### Environmental Commitment

UPM has committed to science-based targets for climate action and aims to achieve carbon neutrality across its operations. The company's entire value chain is built on renewable wood feedstocks, and all UPM products are recyclable or biodegradable. The company invests heavily in closed-loop water systems and zero-waste manufacturing.
