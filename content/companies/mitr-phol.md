## Overview

Mitr Phol is Thailand's largest sugar producer and the world's third-largest, headquartered in Bangkok. Founded in 1946, the company operates across multiple continents with 10,000+ employees. Beyond sugar production, Mitr Phol is strategically expanding into bioplastics manufacturing, leveraging its extensive agricultural biomass feedstocks and sugar production expertise to develop sustainable biodegradable plastic alternatives.

The company is positioning bioplastics as a pillar of structural reform, recognizing the growing market demand for sustainable packaging solutions to replace petroleum-based plastics.

### Core Business: Sugar & Bioplastics

**Traditional Sugar Production:**
- Thailand's largest sugar producer
- World's third-largest sugar producer
- Extensive sugar cane production and processing capabilities
- Established supply chain and agricultural expertise

**Bioplastics Development:**
- Biodegradable plastic production from sugar and starch
- Sustainable alternatives to petroleum-based plastics
- Food packaging applications
- Industrial-scale manufacturing capabilities

### Strategic Partnerships

**Marubeni Collaboration (Japan):**
- Joint venture for bioplastics production and sales
- Focus on bags from sugar cane and starch feedstocks
- Initial sales target: >10 billion yen ($9 million) annually
- Distribution across Japanese and Asian markets

**Braskem Siam Partnership:**
- Ethanol supply agreement with Braskem Siam (joint venture of Braskem & SCG Chemicals)
- Bio-ethylene plant development in Thailand
- Planned capacity: 200,000 tons/year of bio-ethylene
- First bio-ethylene plant in Asia
- Expected completion: 2027
- Feedstock: Agricultural ethanol from Mitr Phol Bio Fuel subsidiary

### Sustainability Commitments

Mitr Phol's bioplastics initiative supports:
- **Plastic Replacement:** Transitioning from fossil fuel plastics to bio-based alternatives
- **Agricultural Valorization:** Leveraging biomass byproducts

- **Carbon Reduction:** Bio-based alternatives with lower carbon footprint
