## Overview

Genomatica is a San Diego-based biotechnology company that has pioneered the use of biological engineering to produce sustainable chemicals from renewable feedstocks. Founded in 2000, the company has developed proprietary fermentation and bioconversion technologies that replace traditional petrochemical processes with bio-based manufacturing routes for high-volume industrial chemicals.

Genomatica's technology platform uses engineered microorganisms to convert plant-based sugars into valuable chemical building blocks. These bio-based chemicals are chemically identical to their fossil-based counterparts, enabling drop-in replacement in existing manufacturing processes and supply chains.

The company's approach combines advanced metabolic engineering, fermentation process development, and catalytic conversion to create economically competitive bio-based alternatives to petroleum-derived chemicals.

## Core Products and Technologies

### Bio-Based 1,4-Butanediol (BDO)

Genomatica's flagship product is bio-based 1,4-Butanediol (BDO), a key chemical intermediate used in the production of polyesters, polyurethanes, and other polymers. BDO is a critical building block for:

- **PBT (Polybutylene Terephthalate)**: Engineering plastic used in automotive and electronics
- **Polyurethanes**: Foams, coatings, and adhesives
- **THF (Tetrahydrofuran)**: Solvent and precursor to spandex fibers
- **GBL (Gamma-Butyrolactone)**: Industrial solvent

Genomatica's bio-BDO is produced through direct fermentation of plant sugars using engineered E. coli, eliminating the need for petroleum-derived acetylene or butane as feedstock. The process has been commercialized through partnerships with major chemical companies.

### Bio-Nylon Intermediates

Genomatica has developed bio-based routes to nylon intermediates, including hexamethylenediamine (HMDA) and adipic acid, which are used in the production of nylon 6,6. These bio-nylon intermediates enable the production of sustainable nylon for textiles, automotive parts, and industrial applications.

The company's bio-nylon technology has been adopted by major sportswear and fashion brands seeking to reduce the environmental impact of their products. Genomatica's bio-based HMDA is chemically identical to fossil-based HMDA, enabling seamless integration into existing nylon production processes.

### Branched Alcohols and Specialty Chemicals

Genomatica has expanded its product portfolio to include bio-based branched alcohols and other specialty chemicals used in personal care, cosmetics, and industrial applications. These products offer improved sustainability profiles while maintaining the performance characteristics required by formulators and manufacturers.

## Technology Platform

### Metabolic Engineering

Genomatica's core competency is the design and optimization of microbial strains for chemical production. The company uses advanced computational modeling, high-throughput screening, and genetic engineering to develop microorganisms that efficiently convert renewable feedstocks into target chemicals.

### Fermentation Process Development

The company has developed proprietary fermentation processes that achieve high yields, titers, and productivity for bio-based chemical production. These processes are designed for commercial-scale manufacturing and have been validated at industrial scale through partnerships with major chemical producers.

### Catalytic Conversion

For some products, Genomatica combines biological fermentation with catalytic conversion steps to produce chemicals that cannot be made directly through fermentation. This hybrid approach expands the range of chemicals that can be produced from renewable feedstocks.
