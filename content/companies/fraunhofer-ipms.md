## Overview

Fraunhofer IPMS (Institut für Photonische Mikrosysteme / Institute for Photonic Microsystems) is a leading international research and development service provider headquartered in Dresden, Germany. The institute specializes in electronic and photonic microsystems, serving industries ranging from smart manufacturing to healthcare and mobility.

As part of the globally distributed Fraunhofer Society, IPMS conducts applied research bridging fundamental science with industrial-scale technology development, including work on sustainable microelectronics and advanced materials.

### Core Research Capabilities

**MEMS & Microsystems:**
- Micro-electromechanical systems design and fabrication
- Custom miniaturized sensors and actuators
- MOEMS (Micro-Opto-Electro-Mechanical Systems)
- Integrated microsystem solutions

**Integrated Circuits & Microelectronics:**
- Application-specific integrated circuits (ASICs)
- Wireless and wired communication systems
- Analog and mixed-signal design
- Power management circuits

**Photonic Systems:**
- Microdisplays and projection systems
- Optical MEMS
- Photonic integration
- Imaging and sensing systems

**Green & Sustainable Microelectronics:**
- Low-power circuit design
- Energy-efficient systems
- Sustainable manufacturing processes
- Materials for green electronics

### Manufacturing Infrastructure

**Advanced Cleanrooms:**
- 1,500 m² of MEMS and MOEMS cleanrooms
- Industry-standard equipment and processes
- 200 mm substrate capability
- From process development to pilot production

**Characterization & Testing:**
- Materials characterization capabilities

### Bioplastics & Materials Connection

While IPMS's primary focus is on microelectronics and MEMS, the institute contributes to bioplastics research through:
- **Materials characterization** of advanced polymers including bioplastics
- **Electrical properties** analysis of bio-based materials
- **Manufacturing process research** for sustainable electronics packaging
- **Collaboration** with other Fraunhofer institutes on bio-based materials (e.g., Fraunhofer UMSICHT)
