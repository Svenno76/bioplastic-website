## Overview

Ecovance Vietnam represents a transformative investment in Southeast Asia's bioplastics capacity and positioning, developing what is anticipated to become the world's largest PBAT (polybutylene adipate terephthalate) biodegradable plastic manufacturing facility. A joint venture of South Korea's SKC Group, Daesang, and LX International, Ecovance Vietnam embodies the convergence of advanced South Korean biopolymer technology with Vietnam's emerging manufacturing capabilities and government support for sustainable materials development.

Located in the Dinh Vu Industrial Zone in Hai Phong City, the facility represents an investment of approximately USD 100 million and is designed to produce 70,000 metric tons of PBAT annually alongside 59,500 metric tons of PBS (polybutylene succinate), establishing Vietnam as a critical global hub for biodegradable polyester production. With operations expected to commence in September 2025 following construction completion in June 2025, Ecovance Vietnam will serve regional and global markets for compostable packaging and sustainable material applications.

The company's focus on PBAT and PBS—two of the most commercially significant biodegradable polyester platforms—positions Ecovance Vietnam to address rapidly growing global demand for compostable packaging and sustainable alternatives to conventional plastics, supporting Vietnam's emergence as a manufacturing center for advanced sustainable materials.

## PBAT and PBS Biodegradable Polyester Technology

Ecovance Vietnam specializes in two of the most commercially significant biodegradable polyester platforms, each serving distinct market segments and application requirements.

### PBAT (Polybutylene Adipate Terephthalate) Technology

**Material Composition:**
- Polyester synthesized from adipic acid and terephthalic acid components
- Butylene glycol linking polymer chains
- Full biodegradability in industrial composting conditions
- Petroleum-derived chemical composition with complete biodegradation
- Performance characteristics comparable to conventional polyethylene

**Key Characteristics:**
- Rapid biodegradation in industrial composting facilities
- Complete conversion to biomass, CO₂, and water
- No persistent environmental accumulation
- Thermal processing compatibility with conventional equipment
- Food contact compliance where applicable

**Performance Advantages:**
- Mechanical properties suitable for thin-film and flexible packaging
- Processing characteristics compatible with existing machinery
- Temperature stability across application ranges
- Strain tolerance and elongation performance
- Processability and conversion efficiency

**Manufacturing Process:**
- Polyester synthesis from adipic acid and terephthalic acid
- Controlled polymerization and molecular weight control
- Quality assurance and product consistency
- Batch and continuous processing capabilities
- Advanced quality control systems

### PBS (Polybutylene Succinate) Technology

**Material Composition:**
- Polyester synthesized from succinic acid and butylene glycol
- Fully biodegradable polyester structure
- Industrial and home composting compatibility
- Petroleum or bio-based feedstock flexibility
- Performance characteristics distinct from PBAT

**Performance Characteristics:**
- Higher thermal stability vs. PBAT
- Enhanced mechanical properties and stiffness
- Suitable for rigid and semi-rigid applications
- Improved barrier properties vs. PBAT
- Distinct application envelope vs. PBAT

**Applications:**
- Rigid packaging and containers
- Semi-rigid applications requiring higher stiffness
- Automotive and industrial components
- Agricultural films and specialized applications
- Thermal processing demanding applications

**Comparative Advantages:**
- Complementary performance to PBAT
- Broader application range vs. single-material approach
- Capability to serve diverse customer requirements
- Differentiation vs. single-product manufacturers
- Flexibility in market positioning and customer offerings
