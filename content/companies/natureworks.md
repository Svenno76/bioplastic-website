## Overview

NatureWorks is the world's leading producer of polylactic acid (PLA) biopolymer, marketed under the proprietary brand name Ingeo. Headquartered in Plymouth, Minnesota (Minneapolis area), NatureWorks was founded in 1997 and has established itself as the global market leader in PLA production and commercialization. With 201-500 employees, the company manufactures a portfolio of PLA resins and compounds designed to serve diverse applications while leveraging renewable resources and delivering performance equivalent to conventional petroleum-based plastics.

NatureWorks' pioneering work in bringing PLA from laboratory innovation to commercial scale production has enabled the widespread adoption of bioplastics across multiple industries. The Ingeo brand represents a well-established, trusted, and widely-certified bioplastic solution used by major brands worldwide.

### Ingeo PLA Technology

**Ingeo Biopolymer Portfolio**: NatureWorks produces a comprehensive range of PLA grades and formulations
- **Ingeo Resin**: Base PLA polymer resins for injection molding, blow molding, and film extrusion
- **Ingeo Compounds**: Specialized PLA formulations with additives for enhanced performance
- **Ingeo Fibers**: Specifically developed PLA fiber grades for textile and apparel applications
- **Ingeo 3D Printing Materials**: PLA filaments and pellets for additive manufacturing

**Key Ingeo Characteristics**:
- Produced from renewable resources (primarily corn and other plant-based feedstocks)
- Biobased content verified and certified
- Industrial and home compostable grades available (certified to EN 13432)
- Performance properties comparable to conventional plastics (PE, PP, PET, PS)
- Lower carbon footprint than petroleum-based polymers
- Suitable for food contact applications (FDA compliant)
- Compatible with existing processing equipment

### Renewable Sourcing

**Plant-Based Feedstocks**: Ingeo is produced primarily from agricultural resources
- Corn and other plant-based carbohydrates serve as primary feedstocks
- Renewable annual crops provide sustainable sourcing
- Fermentation process converts plant sugars to lactic acid
- Advanced production techniques synthesize PLA from lactic acid

**Sustainability Benefits**:
- Reduces dependency on fossil fuels
- Lower greenhouse gas emissions compared to conventional plastics
- Life cycle assessments demonstrate environmental benefits
- Supports circular bioeconomy principles
