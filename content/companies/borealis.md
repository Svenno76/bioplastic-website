## Overview

Borealis is a leading European producer of polyolefins and specialty plastics, headquartered in Vienna, Austria. Founded in 1994 as a joint venture between Statoil (Norway) and Neste (Finland), the company relocated to Vienna in 2006 and is now a second-largest polyolefin producer in Europe. With approximately 6,200 employees, Borealis operates production facilities and offices across more than 40 countries, serving customers globally.

The company is actively transitioning toward bio-based and circular economy solutions, developing innovative polymers incorporating renewable and recycled content while maintaining superior performance characteristics demanded by modern industrial applications.

### Primary Materials & Products

**Bio-Based Polyolefins:**
- **Bio-PE (Bio-Polyethylene)**: Drop-in polyethylene from renewable feedstocks
- **Bio-PP (Bio-Polypropylene)**: Bio-based polypropylene for packaging and industrial applications
- Certified renewable content polymer products
- Bio-circular polyolefin blends incorporating both renewable and recycled content

**Circular Economy Polymers:**
- Recycled polyolefins from post-consumer and post-industrial waste
- Advanced circular polymers for closed-loop applications
- Enhanced recyclable polymer formulations

**Specialty Plastics:**
- Advanced polyethylene grades (LDPE, LLDPE, HDPE)
- Polypropylene and PP compounds
- Engineering thermoplastics
- Specialty film and extrusion materials

### Market Segments

Borealis serves diverse industrial sectors with tailored polymer solutions:
- **Packaging**: Films, containers, and protective packaging for food and industrial goods
- **Automotive**: Components, under-hood applications, lightweight solutions
- **Construction & Building**: Infrastructure materials, durable components
- **Electrical & Electronics**: Cable insulation, device housings, components
- **Consumer Goods**: Household products, durable goods applications
- **Industrial Applications**: Specialized polymers for machinery and manufacturing

### Sustainability & Innovation Strategy

**Bio-Based Material Development:**
Borealis is committed to incorporating renewable feedstocks through:
- Partnerships with sustainable feedstock suppliers
- Development of advanced bio-polymerization technologies
- Integration of renewable content into mainstream polymer products

**Circular Economy Focus:**
The company advances circular principles through:
- Recycled content polymer development
- Post-consumer waste collection and processing
- Chemical and mechanical recycling initiatives
- Product design for recyclability

**Research & Development:**
Borealis operates multiple R&D centers focused on:
- Bio-based polymer chemistry and processing
- Advanced circular polymer technologies
- Performance enhancement of bio-derived and recycled materials
- Sustainable manufacturing process optimization

### Global Operations

Borealis maintains a significant production and technical infrastructure:
- **European Hub**: Extensive polyolefin production capacity with multiple facilities
- **Asian Operations**: Growing manufacturing presence for regional market serving
- **Americas Operations**: Production facilities supporting North American markets
- **R&D Centers**: Research facilities in Austria, Finland, and other key locations

### Strategic Partnerships

Borealis collaborates with research institutions, feedstock providers, and industrial partners to advance bio-based and circular polymer solutions, ensuring cutting-edge innovation and sustainable material development.
