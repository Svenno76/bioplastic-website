## Overview

Notpla is a London-based biotechnology company headquartered in Hackney Wick, United Kingdom. Founded in 2019 by co-founders Pierre Paslier and Rodrigo Garcia Gonzalez (Imperial College London graduates), Notpla develops innovative seaweed-based, plastic-free packaging solutions. The company manufactures 100% biodegradable, edible packaging that replaces conventional plastic containers and wrapping, with particular focus on foodservice and single-use plastic replacement.

Notpla won The Earthshot Prize in 2022 (prestigious global sustainability award in the "Build a Waste-free World" category) and partners with major food industry players to scale sustainable packaging solutions.

### Core Technology: Seaweed-Based Bioplastics

**Seaweed Feedstock:**
- Brown seaweed/brown algae sourcing
- Renewable, fast-growing resource
- No fertilizers, fresh water, or agricultural land required
- Absorbs CO₂ during growth (carbon sequestration)
- De-acidifies and re-oxygenates ocean waters

**Material Properties:**
- 100% naturally biodegradable composition
- Completely edible formulation
- Plastic-free and toxin-free
- Breaks down within weeks in natural environments
- Food-contact safe materials

**Manufacturing:**
- Proprietary seaweed processing technology
- Sustainable conversion methods
- Scalable production processes
- Quality-controlled formulations

### Flagship Product: Ooho

**Ooho Liquid Encapsulation:**
- Thin, flexible seaweed-based membrane
- Encapsulates liquids (sauces, drinks, condiments)
- Single-shot encapsulation for portion control
- 100% edible container
- Zero plastic packaging
- Rapid biodegradation

**Development History:**
- Concept developed at Imperial College London (2013)
- Co-founders' original innovation
- Commercialized through Notpla (2019+)
- Now in partnership applications
