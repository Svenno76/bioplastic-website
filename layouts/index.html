Frequently Asked Questions

What are bioplastics?

Bioplastics are plastic materials derived from renewable biomass sources such as corn starch, sugarcane, cellulose, or vegetable oils. They can be biodegradable (like PLA, PHA) or non-biodegradable but bio-based (like bio-PE). The global bioplastics market is projected to reach 28.7 million tonnes by 2031.

What is the difference between bio-based and biodegradable?

Bio-based refers to the source material (renewable biomass), while biodegradable refers to how the material breaks down. Not all bio-based plastics are biodegradable (e.g., bio-PE), and not all biodegradable plastics are bio-based. PLA is both bio-based and industrially compostable.

Which companies produce bioplastics?

Major bioplastics producers include NatureWorks (PLA), Novamont (Mater-Bi), BASF (ecoflex®), TotalEnergies Corbion (PLA), Braskem (bio-PE), Danimer Scientific (PHA), and Arkema (Rilsan®). Our portal tracks 237+ companies across the entire value chain.

How big is the bioplastics market?

The global bioplastics market was valued at approximately $10.5 billion in 2024 and is projected to grow at a CAGR of 18-20% through 2031, driven by EU regulations, corporate sustainability targets, and consumer demand for eco-friendly packaging.

How do I identify different types of bioplastics?

Common bioplastics include PLA (polylactic acid) for packaging, PHA (polyhydroxyalkanoates) for marine applications, PBAT for flexible packaging, starch blends for disposable items, and bio-PE for rigid containers. Each has distinct properties suited to specific applications.
